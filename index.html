My Education

I'm a part of the Tufts University class of 2018, pursuing degrees in mathematics and computer science. I'm a teaching assistant for Comp 105 (Programming Languages), which teaches students functional programming, in addition to some interesting projects including a type infrencer and lambda calculus interpreter. I'm also a project manager for JumboCode, which empowers students to take on large scale projects to help nonprofits in the Greater Boston area. In addition, I'm a part of Theta Chi Fraternity, and volunteer with the College Diabetes Network and the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation.

My Background

I prefer working on back-end problems, developing and implementing algorithms to solve interesting problems. I've done a lot of work in data parsing and visualization and mathematical modeling, as well as some web development. Some of my more interesting coursework includes concurrency, which focused on both the Actor model of concurrency as well as threading, and Computational Biology. Although I don't have a strong biological background, I find the computational algorithms extremely fascinating and applicable to a wide range of fields.
I've worked with: Python, C,C++, Java, Ruby, HTML/CSS, Erlang, Elixir, Bash, and Standard ML, in addition to a wide variety of software such as MATLAB, Mathematica, Git, Bootstrap, Ruby on Rails, Django, and Heroku.
